The Meteorite Hunter
More than two decades ago, a geologist traveled to Argentina to hunt for hidden craters. What he uncovered could change how we look for extraterrestrial life.

Published on May 9, 2017

Undiscovered is a new podcast about the left turns and lucky breaks that make science REALLY happen. Coming soon from Science Friday.

Annie Minoff is a producer and co-host of Undiscovered. Her favorite stories draw on her love for both art and science. Annie’s first run-in with radio was as an undergrad at Columbia University, where she covered the New York arts scene for the universe’s best radio station, WKCR-FM (“Sit Back and Dig the Shellac”). From there, it was on to Chicago’s WBEZ-FM, where Annie was an assistant producer for the world’s only rock ‘n’ roll talk show, Sound Opinions. In 2013, Annie moved back to NYC to join the SciFri staff. Favorite SciFri topics include bioart, the history of tech, and science fiction.

Original art by Claire Merchlinksy.

Elah Feder is a producer and co-host of Undiscovered and Science Friday’s resident Canadian. She first got into podcasts in 2007 when she was a grad student studying evolutionary biology at the University of Toronto. Working late nights in the lab, sorting fruit flies under a microscope, Elah would listen to a lot of Gwen Stefani. After some noise complaints, she phased out the beats, discovered radio and podcasts, and was never the same. She has contributed segments to CBC Radio shows like Spark, The Current, and The Sunday Edition. Before joining SciFri, Elah investigated the oil and gas industry with the Energy & Environment Reporting Project at the Columbia Journalism School.
